General Guild Rules

1. Zero Tolerance for Trouble

Troublemakers, such as Drama Queens/Kings, Godly Self-Centered Ego Maniacs, and Constant Complainers about game-related and real-life issues, will face permanent bans. Our first rule exists by design, and these individuals are unwelcome.

2. Off-Limit Topics

In the interest of fostering a harmonious environment, we discourage discussions related to politics, religion, or any other contentious real-life subjects within both our Discord and guild chat.

3. Respect and Appreciation

Demonstrating respect for fellow guild members and acknowledging each other's contributions is paramount. While those above Class Master may have specific responsibilities, no one is under absolute obligation. Our primary purpose is to have fun and, if possible, forge friendships.

4. Reporting Issues and Suggestions

We encourage open communication and problem-solving. Rather than escalating disputes, report problems through designated channels. If, for instance, you observe any abuse of power, it will be investigated, even if the offender holds a high-ranking position. Suggest improvements via a dedicated channel, with ideas considered, voted upon, and potentially integrated into our gameplay.

5. English as the Official Language

To maintain coherence, please use English in guild chat and during guild activities, including raids and battlegrounds.

6. Upholding Reputation

As a guild with mature, discerning members who value future collaborations, it's essential to preserve a positive reputation on the server. Practice politeness and respect when interacting with individuals outside the guild. While perfection is not expected, minimize confrontations, and report issues rather than engaging in them.

7. Fun Is Mandatory

In our guild, fun is a requirement, not an option. Engage in various activities, including PvE, PvP, guild chat interactions, or simply relaxing in Stormwind/Ironforge. Find your source of enjoyment and embrace it.

8. Active Participation

Active members must actively participate in guild suggestions and elections. All aspects, except general rules and the loot/rank systems, are subject to change. If a live vote occurs, and a majority of active members (everyone above "Member") approves the suggestion, it becomes an official policy or rule at the start of the second week.

9. Guild Rules as Sacred Laws

In over two millennia of human history, we've learned that the realm of imagination is boundless, especially in the absence of consequences. Rest assured, we'll uphold the sanctity of our guild rules. Violators will be held accountable, with sanctions, warnings, or even permanent bans depending on the gravity of the situation.

10. Application Protocol

Every prospective member must complete an application form for guild entry. Further information on this requirement can be found here.

Additional rules regarding TUS Gladiator

1. Mandatory Live Communication

Participation in real-time voice communication on Discord is obligatory during guild PvP activities. Effective coordination and communication are vital for our success.

2. Consecutive Loss Policy

Gladiators are expected to maintain a high standard of performance. If a Gladiator loses two consecutive battlegrounds, it results in an automatic demotion. Reflect on the reasons for the losses, adapt, and aim to return to peak performance swiftly.

3. Drama-Free Environment

In our guild, open discussion and self-improvement are encouraged. However, we maintain a zero-tolerance policy for drama. Any grievances or weaknesses should be discussed constructively in the dedicated Discord channel to foster an atmosphere of improvement and collaboration.

Inactivity Policy:

In our effort to maintain an active and vibrant guild, we have implemented the following measures to address inactivity:

  1. 1. Seven or More Days Offline (Below Level 60)

    Players below level 60 who have been inactive for seven or more days will be subject to removal from the guild (gkick).

  2. 2. One Month of Inactivity (Without Prior Notice)

    Players who remain inactive for one month without providing prior notice will also be subject to removal from the guild (gkick).

    3. We value active participation and appreciate members who keep the guild's vitality alive. It's important to communicate any anticipated periods of inactivity to prevent unintended removal from the guild.
