Rank System

Guild Rank and Progression System

Initial Roles

1.0 Troublemaker: The Troublemaker rank is reserved for individuals who have violated guild rules. It results in a temporary suspension from guild chat, Discord, and voice channels until a higher-ranking officer can address the issue. Officers may demote individuals to this rank if they breach guild rules.

1.1 Rookie: The Rookie rank is assigned to newly joined members and will be held for at least two weeks as we become acquainted with you. The date of your invitation will be noted on your public profile.

1.1 Paduan: Equivalent to the Rookie rank for TUS Gladiators.

Member Roles

2.0/3.0 Alt Player: Members above the "Member" rank have the privilege of having Alt Players, which they can use in the second 25-Man Raid.

2.1 Member: To maintain the Member rank, participation in most weekly PvE/PvP raids, dungeon runs, or guild battlegrounds is essential, especially for those inclined toward PvP activities.

2.1 Fighter: Equivalent to the Member rank for TUS Gladiators.

Veteran Roles

3.1 Veteran: Veterans are expected to have mastered two primary professions and actively participate in all weekly PvE/PvP raids. They should possess top-tier gear or be very close to it, considering the current server phase, and meet the requirements set by their Class Officer. Veterans can have up to two Alt Players, including one Main Alt and one regular Alt Player, with no priority over regular members in the second 25-Man Raid or PvP activities. Veterans also gain access to collaborations for teaming up with other top players in BGs, dungeons, and arenas. A minimum of two months of guild playtime is required for promotion to the Veteran rank.

3.1 Gladiator: Similar to the Veteran rank, but with additional requirements and regulations (TUS Gladiator only).

Officer Roles

4.0 Leadership-Alt: Leadership Alts are Alt Players of any officer, whether they are low, mid, or high-level officers. They have the flexibility to have unlimited Alt Players within the guild but only one Main Alt.

4.1 Class-Officer: Class-Officers are typically players with extensive knowledge of their respective class. They must have mastered two primary professions and have best-in-slot gear or gear close to it, corresponding to the server's phase.

4.1 Yoda: Similar to Class-Officer, but with additional requirements and rules (TUS Gladiator only).

4.2 Officer: Mid-level officers include Raid Leaders, responsible for leading raids, PvP Officers, responsible for scheduling weekly fun events and leading guild battlegrounds, and Discord and Guild Bank Officers, responsible for managing the guild's Discord server and guild bank in the absence of higher-ranking officers.

4.2 PvP Guru: Similar to the Officer rank for TUS Gladiators.

4.3 Chief-Officer: The Chief-Officer holds a self-explanatory role and essentially shares leadership responsibilities with the guild leader. In the leader's absence, the Chief-Officer has the authority to act as a substitute and exercise leadership.

It's worth noting that the Officer ranks lacks specific characteristics because most individuals attaining this rank will be selected by the community. The determination of our Officer Team will be achieved through elections as we become better acquainted. During this period, I will handle most aspects of guild management, so your understanding and trust are greatly appreciated. The faster trust is established, the sooner I can delegate responsibilities to you.
